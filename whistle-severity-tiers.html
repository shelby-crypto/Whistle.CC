Why this matters for Whistle's severity system: Competitor apps treat direct physical threats as the top tier and rank everything else below. For women and LGBTQ+ athletes, identity-based abuse — racism, sexism, sexualized degradation, homophobia, coordinated harassment — constitutes 65-78% of all incidents they face. It causes documented psychological harm (53% depression/anxiety rate per SafeSport), and platforms largely ignore it (87% of non-racist abuse stays live per FIFA). A severity system that places these incidents below direct threats replicates the gap Whistle is built to close.

Immediate Action

Active danger requiring intervention now. Includes direct threats and active identity-based campaigns causing acute harm. A coordinated racist pile-on warrants the same urgency as a single direct threat.

Harm categories: Direct ThreatsStalkingDoxxingActive Pile-onsMass Sexualized CampaignsActive Impersonation
Shift: Coordinated identity-based incidents are elevated here. World Athletics classifies 14% of abuse at law-enforcement level — much of it identity-based.

Serious Harm

Sustained abuse causing documented psychological harm. This is where most identity-based incidents live — racism, sexism, and homophobia that platforms leave up but athletes absorb.

Harm categories: Sustained RacismSexualized DegradationHomophobic AbuseTargeted HarassmentRepeat OffendersMisogynoir
Shift: This covers 65-78% of actual abuse. SafeSport: 80.8% of athletes experience anti-inclusive behaviors; 53.1% report depression/anxiety.

Escalating

Patterns forming that may become Serious Harm or Immediate Action. Abuse spikes around events, and 10% of abusers produce 80% of harmful content.

Harm categories: Emerging PatternsEvent-Day SpikesCoded LanguageDogpiling StartingNew Accounts Targeting
Shift: Research shows abuse escalates around competition. Catching repeat offenders at this stage prevents higher-tier incidents.

Monitoring

Low-level signals being tracked. Most won't escalate, but logging builds the pattern evidence needed for higher tiers. No action required.

Harm categories: Isolated IncidentsAmbiguous IntentLow-Frequency NegativityBaseline Tracking
Shift: This tier builds evidence that transforms isolated incidents into visible patterns of sustained abuse over time.

Design Rationale
How the research shaped these decisions
📊
Harm impact over threat imminence
Traditional models rank by "how close is physical danger?" But FIFA data shows 78% of abuse is identity-based, and SafeSport shows 53% of athletes develop depression/anxiety from it. Severity must reflect actual harm, not just physical proximity.
⚡
Coordinated abuse is elevated
Resolver found 10% of abusers produce 80% of content. A coordinated pile-on from organized accounts isn't "Concerning" — it's "Immediate Action." World Athletics flagged 14% of abuse for law enforcement review, much of it identity-based campaigns.
🎯
Action-oriented tier names
"Immediate Action" tells the athlete or their team what to do. "Escalating" tells them something is getting worse. Every tier name answers a question someone actually has, not a taxonomy question they don't.
🏷️
Harm category as context, not hierarchy
Moving the harm category to a secondary label prevents the bias where "Direct Threat" always reads as more urgent than "Sexualized Abuse." Both can appear in any tier — a sustained sexualized campaign can warrant the same urgency as a single direct threat.
🔄
Escalation is a first-class tier
Research shows abuse spikes around match days, penalties, and records. Having an "Escalating" tier means the system surfaces patterns before they become crises — catching the early signs that a pile-on is forming.
🛡️
Built for the athletes Whistle serves
Other apps treat harassment and hate as secondary to direct threats. This system is designed for women and LGBTQ+ athletes, where racism, sexism, and homophobia are the primary incidents — not edge cases.
Research Sources Informing This Design
FIFA Threat Matrix — EURO 2020 + AFCON 2022. 406,987 posts analyzed. 78% of abuse racist or homophobic. 87% of non-racist abuse remains live on platforms.
World Athletics — Budapest 2023. 449,209 posts across 1,344 athletes. 51%+ abuse is racism and sexualized content. 14% classified for law enforcement.
Resolver / Kroll — Online Abuse in Sports 2023. 72,995 harmful items in 6 weeks. 87% of Olympic abuse directed at women. 10% of abusers produce 80% of content.
U.S. Center for SafeSport — 2024. 4,980 participants. 80.8% experienced anti-inclusive behaviors. 53.1% depression/anxiety. Only 10.7% file reports.
SafeSport Bullying Handbook — June 2025. LGBTQ+ youth face higher bullying risk. 82% witnessed homophobic behavior. Athletes with disabilities 2-4x more likely to be abused.
